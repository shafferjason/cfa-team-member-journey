# Operational Skills Track

The other half of the dual-track model. While developmental competencies focus on character and leadership growth, operational skills focus on job knowledge and execution ability.

## How Operational Skills Differ from Developmental Competencies

| | Developmental Competencies | Operational Skills |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | Character and leadership traits | Job knowledge and execution ability |
| **Measured by** | 4-level behavioral scale | Certifications, demonstrated proficiency, measurable performance |
| **Examples** | Caring, Courage, Vision | Station certifications, P&L literacy, scheduling |
| **How developed** | Noticed, named, and expected through daily work | Trained, practiced, and certified |

---

## Team Structure Context

- **4 teams:** FOH Day, FOH Night, BOH Day, BOH Night
- Team members are primarily FOH or BOH
- ~20% are cross-trained but mainly work one side; can assist on easier positions on the other side
- Station certifications follow the scheduling positions (FOH and BOH specific)

---

## Team Member — Operational Skills

**Orientation (Day 1):**
- Handbook information, policies, food safety basics
- Culture introduction, onboarding materials
- All covered in the orientation track (see AppStudio passports system)

**Station Certifications (within first 90 days):**
- 3 station certifications on their side (FOH or BOH)
- Certifications follow the scheduling positions
- Any 3 stations on their side — no required sequence
- FOH stations: Front Counter, Drive-Thru, Dining Room, FOH Open, FOH Close, etc.
- BOH stations: Boards, Biscuits, Breader, Prep, Sandwiches, Nuggets/Strips/Soup, BOH Open, BOH Close, etc.

---

## Trainer — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Already certified on 3+ stations on their side

1. **Train-the-Trainer certification** — Structured course on how to teach (not just do). How to break down a task, how to let someone practice, how to give corrections. The Talent Director owns this curriculum and it must be aligned across all 4 teams.
2. **Certified on the stations they'll train** — Can only train stations they've personally mastered. Doesn't need every station, but must be certified on any station they're training others on.
3. **Orientation delivery** — Can lead or co-lead a new hire orientation session. Knows the handbook content well enough to teach it, not just recite it.
4. **Training standards knowledge** — Knows the "how" behind the station, not just the "what." Why we do it this way. What the common mistakes are. What "good" looks like vs. "good enough."

**Important:** A Certified Trainer on FOH Day and a Certified Trainer on BOH Night go through the same Train-the-Trainer process and meet the same bar — even though they're training different stations on different teams. This is a current gap being addressed; trainer standards have historically been inconsistent across teams.

## Team Leader — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Trainer-level operational skills complete

1. **Certified on most/all stations on their side** — Not just 3, but deep coverage of their FOH or BOH stations.
2. **Basic cross-training on the other side** — A FOH Team Leader needs to understand enough about BOH to know when things are falling behind, and vice versa. Not full certification, but working knowledge.
3. **Shift management** — Opening/closing procedures, shift handoff, break rotation, managing the flow of a rush.
4. **Cash/register management** — Counts, drops, discrepancy handling.
5. **Guest recovery** — How to handle complaints, refunds, service failures. Connects to TL Expectation #4: "Protect the guest experience."
6. **Basic food safety oversight** — Not just doing it right themselves but catching when others aren't.
7. **Labor awareness** — Understanding staffing levels, knowing when to call for help or send someone home.

---

## Asst Director — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Team Leader-level operational skills complete

1. **Cross-trained across both FOH and BOH** — Can't manage strategic operations for the restaurant if you only understand one side. *Note: This is a new standard being implemented. Not all current Asst Directors meet this yet, but all new Asst Directors coming through the runway will.*
2. **Scheduling** — Building and managing the schedule (HotSchedules), understanding labor targets, balancing coverage across dayparts.
3. **Staffing/labor management** — Knowing the numbers: labor cost, productivity targets, when to flex up or down.
4. **Food cost basics** — Understanding waste, inventory impact, cost of goods.
5. **Vendor/supply management** — Ordering, inventory counts, managing delivery schedules.
6. **Systems design** — Building and improving operational workflows — checklists, procedures, handoff processes.
7. **Hiring support** — Screening, interviewing, onboarding coordination (working with the Talent Director).
8. **Basic P&L literacy** — Can read and understand the financial statements, even if not owning them.

---

## Director — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Asst Director-level operational skills complete

1. **Full operational proficiency across FOH and BOH** — Deep understanding of both sides, not just working knowledge.
2. **Full P&L ownership** — Not just literacy but accountability. Understanding what drives the numbers and how to move them.
3. **Scheduling strategy** — Not just building a schedule but designing the staffing model. How many people per daypart, where to invest labor, seasonal adjustments.
4. **Hiring and talent decisions** — Full ownership of who joins and stays on their team. Working with Talent Director on pipeline.
5. **Performance management** — Conducting evaluations, having development conversations, addressing underperformance, making the hard calls on people.
6. **Annual/quarterly planning** — Setting goals for their team or function, forecasting needs, building improvement plans.
7. **CFA compliance and audits** — Understanding brand standards, preparing for and passing audits and evaluations.
8. **Cross-functional coordination** — Working with other Directors to align across teams. Director Expectation #5: "Communicate clearly and create alignment."

## Operator — The 6 Essential Jobs

Source: "The High Performing CFA Operator Role" (coach document). These are the anchored blocks scheduled into the Operator's week — the work that only the Operator can do.

### 1. Strategic Vision
Planning and thought-leadership for the business. Regularly scanning the distant landscape for market trends, variables, threats, and opportunities that need to be monitored and considered to best position the business for growth.

### 2. Leadership Management
Leading, guiding, mentoring, developing, and holding accountable your top most mission-critical Directors.

- **Two main responsibilities:** Organizational responsibilities + Developmental journey/growth plan.
- **Span of control:** Discern carefully how many Directors you can effectively "directly" manage/lead (7-8, potentially scaling to 3-4 as the business grows). Organizational structure must reflect this.
- **Differentiated leadership:** Your leadership of your top circle will be different than your leadership to the rest of the Director team. Invest in developmental growth/journey for the entire Director team, but organizational management is reserved for the small circle of top leaders.

### 3. Organizational Presence / Culture Influencer
- **Personally teach/coach** several of the leadership competency training workshops for your leaders — direct influence in shaping their leader frameworks, mindsets, and most critical skills.
- **"Elbow grease" time** — Regularly work alongside team members on a shift in a position (even just an hour). Role in those moments is ONLY to encourage and build relational connection, NOT to teach or critique. Cadence varies: weekly to every few weeks.
- **New hire touchpoints** — Two touchpoints with all new team members:
  - **Group session:** Share your story and the story of the brand/business personally.
  - **Private 1:1:** Hear the new team member's story, personally get to know them.
  - **Sweet spot window:** After week 3, before/by week 8.

### 4. Fiduciary Oversight
Primary financial responsibility. Because CFA manages cash flow, the Operator's role is mainly reviewing financials (~2x/month) and focusing on planning and projections. No succession planning for ownership — that returns to CFA.

### 5. Support Center Relationship / Influence
Building strategic relationships with CFA, Inc. It's a family-owned business that operates around close, trusted relationships — building bridges with people. Good stewardship for growth/expansion opportunities, and advocating for Directors applying for LDP or Operator.

### 6. Community Engagement / Influence
Building bridges with community leaders. Volunteer to sit on panels, attend city council meetings, set up lunches with local leaders and influencers, church elder role, sit on boards of recognized organizations doing great things in the community.

## Specialized Trainer — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Certified Trainer operational skills complete

1. **Certified on majority of stations on their side** — Broad station coverage to be able to train across their area.
2. **Curriculum design** — Ability to create and refine training materials for their specialty area.
3. **Training assessment** — Ability to evaluate whether a trainee is truly ready for certification (not just going through the motions).
4. **Best practice development** — Identifies and documents better ways to do things in their specialty area.
5. **Cross-trainer coordination** — Works with other Specialized Trainers (across the 4 teams) and the Talent Director to maintain consistency.

---

## Station Specialist — Operational Skills

**Prerequisite:** Certified on the station(s) in their specialty area

1. **Expert-level mastery** of their specific operational zone — the recognized best at this station.
2. **Performance metrics ownership** — Knows and tracks the key metrics for their area (speed, accuracy, quality, guest satisfaction).
3. **Process improvement** — Identifies friction points and implements solutions in their area.
4. **Standard documentation** — Maintains and updates procedures and standards for their specialty.
5. **Area-specific training** — Trains others in their specialty's procedures (overlaps with the Mentoring developmental competency).

## Summary: Operational Skills Build on Each Other

```
TM:          Orientation + 3 station certs (their side)
             |
Trainer:     + Train-the-Trainer cert + orientation delivery + training standards
             |
Team Leader:  + most/all stations (their side) + basic cross-training
             + shift management + cash + guest recovery + food safety + labor
             |
Asst Dir:    + full cross-training (both sides) + scheduling + staffing/labor
             + food cost + vendor management + systems design + hiring + P&L basics
             |
Director:    + full FOH/BOH proficiency + P&L ownership + scheduling strategy
             + hiring/talent + performance mgmt + planning + compliance + coordination
             |
Operator:    The 6 Essential Jobs (Strategic Vision, Leadership Management,
             Culture Influence, Fiduciary Oversight, CFA Relationship, Community)
```
