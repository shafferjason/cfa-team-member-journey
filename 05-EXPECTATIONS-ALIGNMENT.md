# Expectations & Competency Alignment

## Vision & Leadership Directive

**Vision:** Create effortless experiences through intentional care.
**Leadership Directive:** Everyone feels confident and cared for — our team members and our guests.

The directive defines the destination; the framework builds the runway.

---

## Team Leader Expectations (5)

These define what leadership looks like when someone has arrived at the Team Leader tier. They are the graduation target for the developmental competency runway.

1. **Stay present over productive** — Lead with awareness, not just activity. Head up, scanning, delegating so you can lead the shift rather than just work it.
2. **Own the standard in real time** — Catch drift early. What you allow becomes the new normal. Address issues as they happen, not after the shift.
3. **Coach in the moment** — Develop people while the work is happening. Quick corrections, quiet encouragement, and real-time teaching.
4. **Protect the guest experience** — Be the last line of defense. Handle recovery with genuine care — not just fixing the issue, but making guests want to come back.
5. **Keep the team connected** — Bridge the plan and the people. Translate what matters into what's happening right now.

### How TL Expectations Map to Competencies

| TL Expectation | Competencies That Build Toward It |
|---------------|----------------------------------|
| Stay present over productive | Caring (TM), Composure (Team Leader) |
| Own the standard in real time | Coachability (TM), Courage (Team Leader) |
| Coach in the moment | Teamwork (Trainer), Caring deepened (Trainer) |
| Protect the guest experience | Caring (TM), Interpersonal (Team Leader) |
| Keep the team connected | Teamwork (Trainer), Interpersonal (Team Leader) |

By the time someone reaches Team Leader, the developmental track has given them the foundation for each of these five expectations.

### Supporting Tools
- **Mid-Shift Self-Check** — Nine reflective questions Team Leaders can use to recalibrate during a shift.
- **Own It vs. Escalate Framework** — Clarifies decision-making boundaries.
- Both of these can serve as formal graduation criteria for the Team Leader → Team Leader transition.

## Director Expectations (5)

These define what leadership looks like at the Director tier — strategic, systems-oriented.

1. **Remove friction by building better systems** — Fix what causes recurring problems. Clarify standards, design workflows, assign process owners so nothing is left to chance.
2. **See ahead and prepare the organization** — Think beyond today. Forecast staffing, volume, training needs. Set clear rhythms (pre-shifts, check-ins, reviews). Spot issues before they hit the shift.
3. **Develop leaders who create the experience** — Build the leadership pipeline. Coach regularly, help leaders think independently, place them where their strengths shine, and prepare the next group before the need becomes urgent.
4. **Shape the culture through key moments** — Show up for openings, transitions, and peaks. Notice when culture drifts. Model the calm, pace, and care expected of all leaders. Guide Team Leaders without taking over.
5. **Communicate clearly and create alignment** — Keep all leadership lanes (FOH, BOH, Talent) aligned. Share expectations in a way leaders can teach and apply. Follow through so communication builds trust.

### How Director Expectations Map to Competencies

| Director Expectation | Competencies That Build Toward It |
|---------------------|----------------------------------|
| Remove friction by building better systems | Decisions, Results (Asst Dir), Strategy (Director) |
| See ahead and prepare the organization | Vision, Strategy (Director) |
| Develop leaders who create the experience | Mentoring (Asst Dir), Vision (Director) |
| Shape the culture through key moments | Integrity (Asst Dir), Composure (Team Leader), Balance (Director) |
| Communicate clearly and create alignment | Vision (Director), Integrity (Asst Dir), Interpersonal (Team Leader) |

Every Director expectation traces back to competencies developed at earlier tiers. Nothing comes out of nowhere.

## The Key Relationship

> Directors set the stage; Team Leaders bring it to life on the floor.

- Everything below Director builds toward **real-time floor execution**.
- Everything at Director and above builds toward **systems, strategy, and culture that make that execution sustainable**.

---

## Director Feedback Insights (from VLD validation)

Key themes from Director survey that validate the framework's design:

- **Biggest TL gap:** Productivity focus over experience focus (confirmed by all 5 Directors).
- **Director consistency is essential** — TLs can't hold a standard Directors aren't modeling. This reinforces why the Director tier has Vision, Strategy, and Balance.
- **Real-time issue addressing** was identified as the standard that slips most — directly maps to TL Expectation #2 (Own the standard in real time) and the Courage competency at Team Leader.

## CFA 5 Critical Success Factors Alignment

The CSF cascade mirrors the progression:

| CSF | Primary Tier Connection |
|-----|------------------------|
| Leadership | Directors, Operator |
| Talent | Trainers, Asst Directors |
| Customer Experience | Team Members, Team Leaders (floor execution) |
| Sales & Brand Growth | Directors, Operator |
| Financial Stewardship | Asst Directors, Directors, Operator |

The order matters: Leadership → Talent → Customer Experience → Sales & Brand Growth → Financial Stewardship. Our tiers develop people UP that same chain.
