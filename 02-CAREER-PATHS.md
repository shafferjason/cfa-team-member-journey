# Career Paths

## The Core Journey — First 90 Days

Every new team member follows the same path for their first 90 days. No exceptions.

| Milestone | Timing | What It Means |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Orientation | Day 1 | Culture introduction, onboarding, materials issued |
| Active Learning | Week 2 | Hands-on training begins with early practice reps |
| 1st Station Certification | Varies | Foundational skills proven |
| 2nd Station Certification | Varies | Versatility and independence demonstrated |
| 3rd Station Certification | Varies | Mastery across three stations |
| Path Selection Conversation | When ready | Reflection chat with director to discuss strengths, interests, and career goals |

### Key Points
- Everyone starts on the same path.
- The path selection conversation typically happens around the 90-day mark, but there is no hard deadline.
- No penalty for taking more time or for choosing to stay at the Core Contributor level.
- Team members can pause or adjust their journey as life circumstances change.

---

## Path 1: Trainer Path

For team members who enjoy teaching others and helping new hires succeed.

**Progression:**
1. **Certified Trainer** — Complete Train-the-Trainer course, lead orientation sessions for new hires.
2. **Specialized Trainer** — Become a niche expert in a specific area (Drive-Thru, Safety, Marketing, etc.), refine best practices, design curriculum. Works under the Talent Director who oversees training restaurant-wide through Specialized Trainers from each of the 4 teams.

**Best for:** Patient communicators who find fulfillment in developing others and maintaining high standards.

---

## Path 2: Operations Director Path (Team Leadership)

For team members who want to lead and develop their own team while driving operational excellence.

**Progression:**
1. **Team Leader (Operational)** — Run shifts, coach team members, solve problems in real-time.
2. **Team Leader (Specialized)** — Advanced leadership focusing on specific domains like Training, Marketing, or other functional areas.
3. **Assistant Operations Director** — Manage strategic operations: systems, staffing, scheduling.
4. **Operations Director** — Lead and develop a dedicated team, collaborate with other directors, report directly to the Operator.

**Best for:** People-oriented leaders who want to build and develop a team, enjoy coaching and mentorship, and thrive on seeing their team succeed.

---

## Path 3: Specialty Director Path (Functional Expertise)

For team members who want to become functional experts working across the entire organization.

**Progression:**
1. **Team Leader (Operational)** — Same starting point as Operations Director path.
2. **Team Leader (Specialized)** — Same intermediate step.
3. **Assistant Operations Director** — Same intermediate step.
4. **Specialty Director** — Functional expert who partners with all Operations Directors to drive restaurant-wide excellence in a specific area.

**Common Specialty Director Roles:**
- Talent Director — Recruiting, hiring, onboarding, and development across all teams.
- Community Engagement Director — Local partnerships, community events, brand presence.
- Training Director — Training programs, standards, and development restaurant-wide.
- Marketing Director — Brand promotion, campaigns, guest engagement strategies.

**Important:** The choice between Operations Director and Specialty Director is NOT made when entering Rank 2. It is made later, after gaining Team Leader experience.

**Best for:** Strategic thinkers who prefer cross-functional work over direct team management.

---

## Path 4: Station Specialist Path

For team members who find deep satisfaction in becoming the absolute best at a specific operational area.

**Specialization Areas:**
- Dining Room Specialist — Guest experience, cleanliness, atmosphere management.
- Drive-Thru Specialist — Speed, accuracy, customer interaction optimization.
- Kitchen Specialist — Food preparation, quality control, efficiency systems.
- Catering Specialist — Large order management and client relationships.
- Opening/Closing Specialist — System startup/shutdown and security protocols.
- Safety & Compliance Specialist — Health regulations, team safety, audit preparation.

**Responsibilities:**
- Own all aspects of performance in your chosen area.
- Train others in your specialty's procedures and standards.
- Identify improvement opportunities and implement solutions.
- Serve as the go-to resource for area-related questions.

**Best for:** Detail-oriented team members who take pride in mastery and enjoy being the recognized expert.

---

## Path 5: Core Contributor Path

For team members who want to stay steady long-term.

- Remain at Rank 1 as a valued, long-term team member.
- Master multiple stations and roles over time.
- Serve as a cultural anchor and reliable presence for the team.
- Eligible for all recognition and achievement badges.
- Can transition to another path at any time if goals change.

**Best for:** Team members who value stability and consistency.

---

## Path 6: Positive Exit (Alumni Path)

- Exit on great terms with the organization.
- Maintain connection as part of the alumni network.
- Recognized for contributions during tenure.

---

## Corporate & Nationwide Opportunities

Available from any path:

- **Field Talent Staff (FTS)** — Travel nationwide to support and train teams at other locations.
- **Leadership Development Program (LDP)** — 24-36 month immersive leadership experience.
- **Talent Development Program (TDP)** — Three-year corporate rotation across strategic projects.

---

## Journey Flexibility Principles

- No hard deadlines — progress at your own pace.
- Path transfers allowed — switch if goals change.
- Pause and resume — the journey adapts.
- Every path is valued — Core Contributor is not "less than."
- Recognition at every level — badges and milestones for all paths.
