# Development Resources

Structured development kicks in at Team Leader and above. For TM and Trainer tiers, development IS the work — leaders notice, name, and expect growth through daily operations (see 06-MASTERY-THRESHOLDS.md).

## How This Works

**The principle:** People learn differently. Some read. Some watch. Some listen. Some process best by talking it through with AI. Rather than forcing one method, each competency offers a **resource menu** — the leader picks the format that works best for them. Directors should stretch leaders to try new methods occasionally, and AI-guided reflection is encouraged as a development tool across the board.

**Time commitment by tier:**

| Tier | Weekly Development Time | Format |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| Team Leader | ~30 min/week | Self-directed from resource menu + monthly coaching conversation with Director |
| Asst Director | ~45-60 min/week | Self-directed from resource menu + bi-weekly coaching conversation with Director/Operator |
| Director | ~1-2 hrs/week | Self-directed + project-based learning + monthly coaching with Operator |

**How to use this document:**
1. Leader and their evaluator identify the competency to develop (from the mastery thresholds)
2. Leader picks 1-2 resources from the menu in whatever format suits them
3. Leader engages with the resource (~30 min)
4. Leader reflects — using the AI prompt, journaling, or conversation with their evaluator
5. Leader applies — identifies one specific thing to practice on their next shift

## Team Leader Resources

### Interpersonal
*Learning from mistakes, taking responsibility in conflict, restating others' opinions, listening openly to criticism*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Crucial Conversations** — Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, et al. — *Ch. 5 "Make It Safe" and Ch. 7 "STATE My Path"* — The gold standard for navigating high-stakes conversations. Teaches how to create safety, listen actively, and restate others' views. Directly applicable to shift conflicts and guest escalations.
- **Thanks for the Feedback** — Douglas Stone and Sheila Heen — *Ch. 1-3 on the three triggers* — Flips the script: teaches how to receive feedback well. Identifies three triggers (truth, relationship, identity) that cause us to reject criticism.
- **Nonviolent Communication** — Marshall B. Rosenberg — The observation-feeling-need-request framework is practical for restaurant floor conflicts and team dynamics.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Sheila Heen — "How to Use Others' Feedback to Learn and Grow"** (TEDxAmoskeagMillyard) — Explains the three triggers that cause us to dismiss feedback. Short and practical.
- **Jocko Willink — "Extreme Ownership"** (TEDxUniversityofNevada, 2017) — A Navy SEAL commander shows how taking full ownership — not blame-shifting — transforms leadership.
- **Kim Scott — "How to Lead with Radical Candor"** (TEDxPortland) — The Care Personally + Challenge Directly framework for giving and receiving honest feedback.

**Podcasts:**
- **Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast — "How Effective Leaders Handle Criticism, Parts 1 & 2"** — Breaks down types of critics and how to respond. ~30 min each.
- **Radical Candor Podcast** (Kim Scott, Jason Rosoff, Amy Sandler) — Ongoing series on caring personally while challenging directly. Episodes are 20-40 min.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Team Leader at a Chick-fil-A restaurant. Recently, [describe a situation where you received criticism or had a conflict]. Help me walk through how I could have responded better using the frameworks from 'Crucial Conversations' (making it safe) and 'Thanks for the Feedback' (identifying my triggers). Then help me prepare what I would say to go back to that person and have a productive conversation."

### Courage
*Surfacing issues others avoid, speaking directly on controversial issues, being courageous in meetings*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Dare to Lead** — Brené Brown — *Part One: "Rumbling with Vulnerability"* — Defines courage as a teachable skill set. Emphasizes that "daring leaders are never silent about hard things." Directly applicable to speaking up about team issues and addressing underperformance.
- **Extreme Ownership** — Jocko Willink and Leif Babin — *Ch. 2 "No Bad Teams, Only Bad Leaders" and Ch. 10 "Leading Up and Down the Chain of Command"* — The courage required to own every problem on your team and speak truth to your chain of command.
- **The Challenge Culture** — Nigel Travis (former CEO of Dunkin' Donuts) — Argues that healthy organizations require people at every level to challenge ideas respectfully. Practical guidance from the QSR world.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Brené Brown — "The Power of Vulnerability"** (TED, 2010) — Connects vulnerability to courage. The willingness to be uncomfortable is the foundation of brave leadership. 60M+ views.
- **Brené Brown — "Listening to Shame"** (TED, 2012) — Goes deeper into how shame prevents us from speaking up and how courage requires working through it.

**Podcasts:**
- **Dare to Lead with Brené Brown — "The Heart of Daring Leadership"** — Unpacks the first courage skill set: rumbling with vulnerability.
- **Andy Stanley Leadership Podcast — "Courageous Leadership, Part 1"** — Explores why people avoid hard conversations and what establishes someone as a leader.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Team Leader and I've noticed a pattern on my team that I think needs to be addressed, but I'm nervous about bringing it up because [describe your concern]. Using Brené Brown's 'rumbling with vulnerability' framework and Jocko Willink's principle of 'leading up the chain of command,' help me draft what I would say to surface this issue constructively. Frame it as ownership rather than blame."

### Composure
*Staying composed under pressure, being a calming influence, handling stress well*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Stillness Is the Key** — Ryan Holiday — Draws on Stoic philosophy to show that inner calm is a leadership weapon. Short chapters on figures like Churchill and Fred Rogers who maintained composure under extreme pressure. Ideal for Team Leaders with limited reading time.
- **Emotional Intelligence** — Daniel Goleman — *Part Three: "Emotional Intelligence Applied" (self-regulation section)* — The foundational book on EQ. Self-regulation — controlling impulses and maintaining calm — is directly relevant to leading during a dinner rush or difficult guest interaction.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Will Guidara — "The Secret Ingredients of Great Hospitality"** (TED) — Staying present and creating space for excellence even under pressure. Directly relevant to your hospitality context.
- **Daniel Goleman — "Why Aren't We More Compassionate?"** (TED, 2007) — How being present with others rather than reactive leads to better outcomes.

**Podcasts:**
- **The Daily Stoic with Ryan Holiday** — Short daily episodes (5-10 min) on staying calm and making clear decisions. Perfect for a Team Leader's commute.
- **Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast — "How Effective Leaders Handle Criticism, Part 2"** — Maintaining composure when receiving criticism.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Team Leader at a Chick-fil-A and I sometimes lose my composure during the lunch rush when things go wrong — [describe a recent situation]. Using Ryan Holiday's Stoic principles and Daniel Goleman's self-regulation framework, help me build a personal 'composure protocol' — 3-5 mental practices I can use in the moment to stay calm, think clearly, and be a calming influence on my team."

## Asst Director Resources

### Mentoring
*Creating development plans, building/developing people, giving growth-focused feedback*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **The Coaching Habit** — Michael Bungay Stanier — Built around 7 essential questions that turn any conversation into a coaching moment in 10 minutes or less. Perfect for developing people in brief interactions between rushes. *Key questions: "What's on your mind?" and "What was most useful for you?"*
- **Radical Candor** — Kim Scott — The Care Personally + Challenge Directly operating system for growth-focused feedback. Distinguishes between Radical Candor, Ruinous Empathy, Obnoxious Aggression, and Manipulative Insincerity.
- **Multipliers** — Liz Wiseman — Research on 150+ leaders showing that "Multipliers" get 2x more from their people. The "Investor" discipline is specifically about giving ownership and accountability to grow people.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Kim Scott — "How to Lead with Radical Candor"** (TEDxPortland) — The 2x2 framework for feedback that develops rather than deflates.
- **Liz Wiseman — TEDx on Multipliers** — How to shift from a Diminisher to a Multiplier leader.
- **Will Guidara — "The Secret Ingredients of Great Hospitality"** (TED) — Demonstrates mentoring through high expectations and genuine care.

**Podcasts:**
- **WorkLife with Adam Grant — "The Three Big Myths of Mentoring"** — Debunks common misconceptions about mentoring relationships.
- **Radical Candor Podcast** (Kim Scott) — Ongoing practical examples of growth-focused feedback.
- **Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast** — Regularly covers developing leaders and giving feedback that builds confidence. ~30 min episodes.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm an Assistant Director at a Chick-fil-A and I need to create a 90-day development plan for a Team Leader who has strong operational skills but struggles with [specific area]. Using 'The Coaching Habit' (the 7 questions) and 'Radical Candor' (Care Personally + Challenge Directly), help me: (1) design a plan with specific milestones, (2) draft the conversation I'd have to present the plan, and (3) suggest 2-3 coaching questions for our weekly check-ins."

### Integrity
*Being a role model for your vision, personal behavior consistent with values, holding values in good and bad times*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Leaders Eat Last** — Simon Sinek — The "Circle of Safety" concept: leaders sacrifice comfort for those in their care. Explores trust at a biological and anthropological level.
- **Setting the Table** — Danny Meyer (founder of Union Square Hospitality Group / Shake Shack) — The distinction between service (what you do) and hospitality (how you make people feel). Values-based leadership from the restaurant industry.
- **Good to Great** — Jim Collins — *Ch. 2 "Level 5 Leadership"* — Level 5 leaders take the blame, give away the credit, and quietly obsess over making the organization great. The research-backed definition of integrity in leadership.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Simon Sinek — "How Great Leaders Inspire Action"** (TED, 2009) — The Golden Circle: leaders who communicate from purpose first earn trust and inspire action. 60M+ views.
- **Simon Sinek — "Why Good Leaders Make You Feel Safe"** (TED, 2014) — How leaders who create a circle of safety earn their people's trust.

**Podcasts:**
- **Dare to Lead with Brené Brown — "Living Into Our Values"** — Identifying your two core values, defining specific behaviors that support them, and recognizing what gets in the way. Includes a values exercise.
- **Andy Stanley Leadership Podcast — "Integrity in the Life of a Leader"** — What integrity looks like in the daily life of a leader.
- **How I Built This with Guy Raz — "Shake Shack: Danny Meyer"** — A real-world case study of integrity under growth pressure from the restaurant industry.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm an Assistant Director at a Chick-fil-A and I want to make sure my daily behavior is consistent with the values I talk about with my team. Help me do Brené Brown's 'Living Into Our Values' exercise: (1) Help me identify my top two core values. (2) For each, define 3 observable behaviors that demonstrate that value in a restaurant context. (3) Identify 3 'slippery behaviors' — things I might do under pressure that contradict those values. (4) Create a weekly self-check I can do in 5 minutes every Sunday night."

### Decisions
*Efficient decision-making, making tough decisions, making timely decisions*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Decisive** — Chip Heath and Dan Heath — The WRAP framework: Widen your options, Reality-test your assumptions, Attain distance before deciding, Prepare to be wrong. Research shows people who consider even one additional alternative do 6x better.
- **Essentialism** — Greg McKeown — Ruthlessly eliminate the nonessential to focus on the vital few things. "If you don't prioritize your life, someone else will." Directly applicable to restaurant leaders facing hundreds of micro-decisions daily.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Chip Heath — "How to Make Better Decisions"** (Talks at Google / Stanford) — The WRAP framework with engaging real-world examples. Search "Chip Heath Decisive" on YouTube.

**Podcasts:**
- **EntreLeadership Podcast — "Make Better Decisions With These Key Principles"** (with Dave Ramsey) — Principles for business decisions, including why indecision is worse than a wrong decision.
- **Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast** — Frequently covers decision-making frameworks in practical, accessible episodes.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm an Assistant Director at a Chick-fil-A and I'm facing a tough decision: [describe the decision]. Walk me through the WRAP framework from 'Decisive': (1) Help me Widen my options beyond a simple yes/no. (2) Help me Reality-test my assumptions — what might I be wrong about? (3) Help me Attain distance — what would I advise a friend in this situation? (4) Help me Prepare to be wrong — what's my Plan B?"

### Results
*Seizing opportunities, pursuing results with drive, achieving high-quality results, continuous improvement*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Excellence Wins** — Horst Schulze (co-founder of The Ritz-Carlton) — Dan Cathy (CFA chairman) credits Schulze with the "my pleasure" response. His philosophy: every time you permanently eliminate a mistake, you save money and improve your product simultaneously. Continuous improvement from the hospitality world.
- **Unreasonable Hospitality** — Will Guidara — The story of transforming Eleven Madison Park into the world's best restaurant by relentlessly pursuing results through hospitality. Already referenced in the VLD.
- **The Power of Moments** — Chip Heath and Dan Heath — Four elements that make experiences memorable: elevation, insight, pride, and connection. Practical frameworks for creating peak moments for guests and team members.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Will Guidara — "The Secret Ingredients of Great Hospitality"** (TED) — Pursuing results through unreasonable hospitality.
- **Marcus Buckingham — "Business Case for Strengths"** (Conference Talk) — Teams achieve higher results when leaders help people play to their strengths. Referenced in the VLD.

**Podcasts:**
- **How I Built This with Guy Raz — "Shake Shack: Danny Meyer"** — How to seize opportunities and pursue results while maintaining quality and values.
- **WorkLife with Adam Grant — "How to Build a Great Culture" (with Danny Meyer)** — Building a culture of excellence and care that drives results.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm an Assistant Director at a Chick-fil-A and I want to apply Horst Schulze's continuous improvement philosophy and Will Guidara's 'unreasonable hospitality' framework. Help me: (1) Identify 3 recurring operational friction points I could permanently eliminate this quarter. (2) For each, design a simple process improvement. (3) Brainstorm 2-3 'unreasonable hospitality' moments we could create for our regular guests that would be memorable but realistic for a QSR environment."

## Director Resources

### Vision
*Communicating compelling vision, inspiring others, providing strategic vision, creating alignment*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Start with Why** — Simon Sinek — The Golden Circle (Why, How, What): people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it. Leaders who communicate from purpose first inspire action and alignment.
- **Unreasonable Hospitality** — Will Guidara — A clear vision ("make people feel seen") drove every decision at Eleven Madison Park. Demonstrates how vision creates alignment across an entire team.
- **Peak** — Chip Conley (founder of Joie de Vivre Hospitality / Airbnb culture lead) — Applies Maslow's Hierarchy to business: employees, customers, and investors need more than survival. A compelling vision addresses higher needs.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Simon Sinek — "How Great Leaders Inspire Action"** (TED, 2009) — The definitive talk on communicating vision through purpose. 60M+ views.
- **Will Guidara — "The Secret Ingredients of Great Hospitality"** (TED) — How a clear hospitality vision translates into specific, memorable actions.

**Podcasts:**
- **How Leaders Lead with David Novak** (former CEO of Yum! Brands — KFC, Pizza Hut, Taco Bell) — Top leaders on communicating vision and creating alignment. Direct restaurant/QSR connection.
- **Maxwell Leadership Podcast — "Leader-Vision: How to See and Sculpt the Future"** — How to develop, articulate, and transfer a vision to your team.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Director at a Chick-fil-A and I need to communicate our franchise's vision for the next year to our entire team — from Directors down to new team members. Using Simon Sinek's Golden Circle (Why, How, What) and Will Guidara's approach, help me: (1) Articulate our 'Why' in one clear sentence. (2) Translate that into 3 specific 'How' behaviors every team member can practice daily. (3) Draft a 5-minute vision talk for an all-team meeting that's inspiring but concrete enough that people know what to do differently starting tomorrow."

### Strategy
*Understanding marketplace dynamics, providing strategic direction, being a gifted strategist*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Good to Great** — Jim Collins — The Hedgehog Concept (what you're passionate about + what you can be best at + what drives your economic engine), the Flywheel Effect, and confronting the brutal facts. Practical strategic thinking framework.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** — W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne — Creating new market space rather than competing in crowded "red oceans." Helps think about how to differentiate in the local market beyond just being another QSR.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Jim Collins** — Talks and lectures available at jimcollins.com/media — How great organizations translate strategy into action.
- **Danny Meyer — TEDxManhattan on "Fine Casual"** (2015) — The strategic insight behind combining fine dining hospitality with casual accessibility — the insight that built Shake Shack. Directly relevant to marketplace dynamics in food service.

**Podcasts:**
- **How Leaders Lead with David Novak** — Strategic thinking in competitive restaurant markets from the former CEO of Yum! Brands.
- **How I Built This with Guy Raz — "Shake Shack: Danny Meyer"** — Meyer's strategic journey from fine dining to fast casual, reading marketplace dynamics.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Director at a Chick-fil-A in Texas and I need to think strategically about our restaurant's positioning. Using Jim Collins' Hedgehog Concept and Blue Ocean Strategy's framework, help me: (1) Define our Hedgehog Concept as a franchise. (2) Identify 2-3 areas where we might be in a 'red ocean' and brainstorm 'blue ocean' differentiation. (3) Analyze what marketplace dynamics (delivery apps, labor market, local competition) I should be watching and how they should influence our next 12-month plan."

### Balance
*Balancing work and personal life, finding time for personal reflection*

**Books/Chapters:**
- **Essentialism** — Greg McKeown — Choosing what truly matters and eliminating everything else. "If you don't prioritize your life, someone else will." Gives permission and a framework for saying no.
- **Stillness Is the Key** — Ryan Holiday — Intentionally creating space for reflection, solitude, and rest as leadership practices, not luxuries. Leaders who maintained effectiveness precisely because they built in reflection time.
- **Free to Focus** — Michael Hyatt — A system for cutting, automating, and delegating so leaders spend time on their highest-contribution activities. Designed for overwhelmed leaders who need practical steps to reclaim margin.

**Videos/Talks:**
- **Marcus Buckingham — "Business Case for Strengths"** (Conference Talk) — Spending time on strengths — not just more hours — drives results. Reframes balance: it's not about doing less, it's about doing more of what energizes you. Referenced in the VLD.
- **Brené Brown — "The Power of Vulnerability"** (TED, 2010) — People who live "wholeheartedly" have the courage to set boundaries and say no.

**Podcasts:**
- **Carey Nieuwhof Leadership Podcast — Ep. 571: "Michael Hyatt on the Fallout from Workaholism"** — Real-world consequences of workaholism on family and career. Deeply personal and practical.
- **The Daily Stoic with Ryan Holiday** — Daily 5-10 min episodes on reflection, presence, and perspective. Perfect for building a reflection habit.

**AI Reflection Prompt:**
> "I'm a Director at a Chick-fil-A and I'm struggling with work-life balance. I'm at the restaurant [X] hours a week and I feel guilty when I'm not there. Using Essentialism and 'Free to Focus,' help me: (1) Identify which of my weekly tasks are 'essential' vs. 'nonessential' based on my unique contribution as a Director. (2) Identify 3 things I could delegate to my Assistant Directors. (3) Design a realistic weekly schedule with at least one block for personal reflection and one protected evening for family. (4) Draft a conversation I could have with my Operator about sustainable pace."

## Structured Mentoring Cadence

Beyond self-directed resources, each tier has a structured coaching rhythm:

### Team Leader
- **Weekly:** 15-min check-in with Director (can be during shift overlap — doesn't require a sit-down)
- **Monthly:** 30-min development conversation with Director — review competency growth, discuss what they're learning, identify one area to focus on next month
- **Quarterly:** Formal competency check-in using the mastery thresholds — where are they on the 4-level scale?

### Asst Director
- **Bi-weekly:** 30-min coaching conversation with Director or Operator — deeper than a check-in, focused on one competency area at a time
- **Monthly:** Project review — what systems, processes, or initiatives are they owning? What's the impact?
- **Quarterly:** Formal competency assessment + development plan update

### Director
- **Monthly:** 1-hour coaching conversation with Operator — strategic topics, competency development, leadership challenges
- **Quarterly:** Formal competency assessment + review of team development (how are their leaders growing?)
- **Annually:** Comprehensive review including 360-style feedback from peers and direct reports

## Project-Based Learning

For Asst Director and Director tiers, real projects accelerate development more than any book.

### Asst Director Projects (examples)
- **Mentoring:** Own a Team Leader's 90-day development plan end-to-end
- **Integrity:** Lead a "values reset" conversation with their team after identifying a drift
- **Decisions:** Own a scheduling redesign for one daypart, present recommendations with data
- **Results:** Identify and permanently solve one recurring operational problem, document the before/after

### Director Projects (examples)
- **Vision:** Draft and deliver a quarterly vision talk to the full team
- **Strategy:** Conduct a competitive analysis of the local market and present strategic recommendations
- **Balance:** Design and implement a sustainable staffing model that reduces Director hours without sacrificing results

## Using AI as a Development Tool

Encourage leaders to use Claude (or similar AI) as a thinking partner for their development. Ways to use it:

1. **Scenario practice** — "Here's a hard conversation I need to have. Help me prepare."
2. **Concept exploration** — "Explain the Hedgehog Concept from Good to Great and help me apply it to our restaurant."
3. **Reflection processing** — "Here's what happened on my shift today. Help me think through what I could have done differently."
4. **Development planning** — "I'm working on my Courage competency. Based on my situation, what should I focus on this month?"
5. **Resource discovery** — "I just finished Dare to Lead. What should I read/watch/listen to next for developing Courage?"

**The key principle:** AI is a thinking partner, not a replacement for the work. The leader still has to have the hard conversation, make the decision, and show up on the floor. AI helps them prepare, reflect, and grow faster.
