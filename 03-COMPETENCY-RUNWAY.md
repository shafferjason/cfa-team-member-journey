# Competency Runway — Dual-Track Development Model

## The Model

Every tier in the progression has two parallel tracks that must both be met to advance:

1. **Operational Skills** — Position mastery, performance behaviors, station competency (still to be defined)
2. **Developmental Leadership** — Leadership competencies drawn from the Leadership Circle Profile 360 + one foundational competency (Coachability)

Both tracks run in parallel. A team member must meet the threshold on both to graduate to the next tier.

## The Runway Metaphor

Each tier has a minimum time requirement — the "runway." From coaching:
- Too short and the plane doesn't have enough time to take off (not enough development time).
- Too long and you're wasting fuel (losing momentum, holding back capable people).
- Each runway is deliberately designed.

## Complete Competency Runway

| Tier | Runway | Developmental Competencies | Theme |
|------|--------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Team Member** | 6 months | **Caring**, **Coachability** | Learn the heart |
| **Trainer** | 6 months | **Teamwork**, **Caring** (deepened) | Lead through connection |
| **Team Leader** | 12 months | **Interpersonal**, **Courage**, **Composure** | Lead through character |
| **Asst Director** | 12 months | **Mentoring**, **Integrity**, **Decisions**, **Results** | Lead through accountability |
| **Director** | 12 months (minimum — seat availability dependent) | **Vision**, **Strategy**, **Balance** | Lead through direction |
| **Operator** | Ongoing | **Stewardship**, **Scalability**, **Legacy** | Lead through ownership |

## Why This Order

The competency assignments follow a deliberate progression:

**Team Member — "Learn the heart"**
- Caring: The hospitality foundation. Treat every guest and teammate with dignity. The Will Guidara "feel" piece. This is the seed of everything.
- Coachability: The prerequisite for all growth. If you can't receive feedback and apply it, nothing else develops.

**Trainer — "Lead through connection"**
- Teamwork: Create a climate that supports people doing their best. Share leadership.
- Caring (deepened): Shifts from "how I treat people" to "how I connect with and develop the person I'm training." Deeper relational investment.

**Team Leader — "Lead through character"**
- Interpersonal: Learn from mistakes, own your part in conflict, listen openly to criticism.
- Courage: Surface tough issues, speak directly on controversial topics.
- Composure: Stay composed under pressure, be a calming influence in difficult situations.

**Asst Director — "Lead through accountability"**
- Mentoring: First role responsible for developing the people below you. Help others create development plans, provide growth-focused feedback.
- Integrity: Making decisions that affect people's livelihoods. Values-driven consistency becomes essential.
- Decisions: Handle strategic operations. Make the tough calls efficiently and in a timely manner.
- Results: Accountable for outcomes, not just effort. Drive and continuous improvement.

**Director — "Lead through direction"**
- Vision: Whether Operations or Specialty, a Director sets direction. Articulate where your team or function is headed, create alignment.
- Strategy: No longer just executing — designing the approach. Firm grasp of dynamics, thoroughly thought-through direction.
- Balance: A Director who burns out can't lead. Balance is a senior leadership skill, not a nice-to-have. Matches the operator coach's emphasis on sustainability, the 6 essential jobs, designing white space.

**Operator — "Lead through ownership"**
- Stewardship: Being a faithful owner of what's been entrusted to you. The 6 core jobs, the anchored blocks. Not just running a business, but tending to it responsibly.
- Scalability: Your leadership multiplies without your presence. You've built leaders who build leaders. The restaurant runs — and the culture holds — when you're not there.
- Legacy: The culture you're shaping outlasts any individual. The hospitality ethos, the development paths, the "how we made people feel" piece.

## Competency Source

- **Caring, Teamwork, Interpersonal, Mentoring** — from Leadership Circle "Relating" dimension
- **Composure, Integrity, Courage, Balance** — from Leadership Circle "Being" dimension
- **Vision, Strategy, Results, Decisions** — from Leadership Circle "Achieving" dimension
- **Coachability** — foundational competency (not in Leadership Circle, added for TM tier)
- **Stewardship, Scalability, Legacy** — Operator-level competencies (beyond Leadership Circle, derived from coaching conversations)

## The Progression Logic

Earlier tiers → Relational foundations (Relating)
Middle tiers → Personal character under pressure (Being)
Upper tiers → Strategic leadership and outcomes (Achieving)
Operator → Ownership and multiplication (beyond the framework)

## Trainer Path Competency Runway

The Trainer Path shares the first two stages with the Leadership Path, then diverges.

| Stage | Runway | Competencies | Theme |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------|
| **TM** | 6 months | Caring, Coachability | Learn the heart (shared) |
| **Certified Trainer** | 6 months | Teamwork, Caring (deepened) | Lead through connection (shared) |
| **Specialized Trainer** | 12 months | **Mentoring**, **Interpersonal**, **Courage** | Lead through development |

### Why These Competencies for Specialized Trainer
- **Mentoring** — You're now developing other trainers, not just trainees. Building people's competence so your zone runs well without you.
- **Interpersonal** — Navigating the harder feedback conversations — telling someone they're not ready, working through disagreements about methods.
- **Courage** — Holding training standards even when it's easier to pass someone through. Pushing back on Directors if training is being shortcut.

### Trainer Path Ceiling and Bridge
- The Trainer Path tops out at **Specialized Trainer**. There is no Master Trainer role.
- The person who oversees training restaurant-wide is the **Talent Director** — a Specialty Director on the Leadership Path.
- The Talent Director manages training *through* Specialized Trainers embedded in each of the 4 teams, not instead of them.
- A Specialized Trainer who wants to keep growing in training would bridge into the Leadership Path (Team Leader → Asst Director → Talent Director).
- **Real-world example:** The current Talent Director bridged from Assistant Director to Talent Director — she came through the Leadership Path, not the Trainer Path. Training expertise gives credibility, but leadership competencies qualify someone for a Director seat.

## Station Specialist Competency Track

Station Specialist is a single role with deepening expertise over time — no stages, no fixed runway. It can also coexist with leadership roles (e.g., Team Leaders who are also Station Specialists in catering delivery).

### Competencies (developed over time, no fixed timeline)

| Competency | Why This One |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Courage** | You own the standard in your zone. When shortcuts are being taken — during rush, short-staffed, whatever — you speak up. You're the person who says "that's not how we do it here" for your area. |
| **Results** | Your zone gets measurably better because you're in it. You don't just maintain — you improve. Speed, quality, efficiency, guest experience — whatever the metrics are for your area, you drive them. |
| **Mentoring** | You develop others' skills in your area. Not just showing them once — actually building their competence so your zone runs well even when you're not on shift. |

### Bidirectional Bridge with Leadership Path
- **Specialist → Team Leader:** A Station Specialist who develops Interpersonal and Composure (from the Team Leader tier) can bridge into a Team Leader role. They already have Courage and Mentoring.
- **Team Leader → Specialist:** A Team Leader with deep operational expertise gets the Station Specialist designation on top of their leadership role. Like the current catering delivery Team Leaders who are both.
- **Holding both:** Some people are both, and that's fine. The competency tracks overlap enough that it's recognition of dual strengths, not double the work.

## Core Contributor Track

Core Contributors (Rank 1, long-term) don't have a formal competency runway. There are two types:

### The Growers
Want to stay at Rank 1 but still want to get better. Master more stations, be a stronger teammate, deepen their skills. Not pursuing leadership, but care about their craft.

- **Operational growth** — additional station certifications, deeper mastery in existing stations
- **Recognition** — badge system catches them: Steadfast Contributor, Heart of Hospitality, Determination Award, Dual-Station Mastery, multi-year milestones
- **Informal competency growth** — may naturally develop Teamwork, Courage, or other competencies through experience. Leaders name it when they see it. Not a formal track with evaluations.

### The Steady Hands
Reliable, show up, do the job, go home. Other priorities in life. Perfectly valued.

- Meet the baseline (Caring, Coachability at Level 2 minimum)
- Be reliable
- No development conversations they didn't ask for. No pressure to grow.
- The door is open if they ever want it, but it's never pushed.

### How Leaders Handle the Difference
The key is the 1-on-1 conversation. A simple question: *"What do you want your time here to look like?"* Both "I want to grow" and "I just need a good job with good people" are respected answers.

## Important Context: Director Seat Availability

All Director positions have had 0 turnover in the past 9 years. This means:
- The Asst Director → Director runway of 12 months is the *minimum*, but actual promotion depends on a seat opening.
- The Asst Director role must be fulfilling enough that capable people don't leave while waiting.
- When a Director seat does open, someone is prepared and ready — not scrambling to develop someone.
- Competency development doesn't stop at "graduation ready" — continued growth deepens readiness.
