# Organizational Structure

## Key Roles & Reporting

- **Operator**: Top-level leader of the restaurant. All directors report to the Operator.
- **Operations Director**: Leads a dedicated team of team members and leaders. Collaborates with other Operations Directors to achieve restaurant-wide goals. Reports to the Operator.
- **Specialty Director**: Functional expert (e.g., Talent Director, Community Engagement Director) who works horizontally across all Operations Directors to support the organization as a whole. Has no direct reports but is part of the director team at peer level with Operations Directors. Reports to the Operator.
- **Assistant Operations Director**: Manages strategic operations including systems, staffing, and scheduling. Reports to the Operator.
- **Team Leader (Specialized)**: Advanced Team Leader focusing on a specific domain such as Training, Marketing, or other functional areas. Mentors Operational Team Leaders.
- **Team Leader (Operational)**: First-line leadership role responsible for running shifts, upholding standards, and coaching peers.
- **Trainer (Certified)**: Completed Train-the-Trainer course; leads orientation sessions and teaches new team members.
- **Trainer (Specialized)**: Niche expert in a specific area (Drive-Thru, Safety, Marketing, etc.) who refines best practices and designs curriculum.
- **Station Specialist**: Deep expertise owner of a specific operational zone (Dining Room, Drive-Thru, Kitchen, Catering, etc.).
- **Core Contributor (Team Member)**: Standard team member role. Most team members remain here long-term, and it is a valued, respected position — not a stepping stone.

## Important Structural Notes

- Operations Directors each lead their own dedicated team. They do NOT have restaurant-wide authority individually, but they collaborate with other Operations Directors to achieve store-wide goals.
- Specialty Directors work across all Operations Directors without direct reports. They partner horizontally rather than leading vertically.
- Both Operations Directors and Specialty Directors are peer-level roles reporting directly to the Operator.
- The key difference: **Operations Director** = leads a team vertically (team leadership). **Specialty Director** = partners across all teams horizontally (functional expertise).
- The choice between Operations Director and Specialty Director is NOT made when entering Rank 2. It is made later, after gaining Team Leader experience.

## Rank System

### Rank 1 — Foundation & Core Contributors
- Where most team members live, even long after completing their initial certifications.
- Includes everyone in their first 90 days AND long-term Core Contributors.
- Being at Rank 1 is perfectly fine and valued — it is the stable foundation of the restaurant.

### Rank 2 — Specialized Roles (Requires Promotion Decision)
- Rank 2 is a specific promotion, not an automatic advancement.
- Three Rank 2 variants exist:
  1. **Rank 2 — Trainer**: Certified Trainer working toward Specialized Trainer.
  2. **Rank 2 — Advanced Team Member (Leadership Track)**: On the path toward Team Leader and eventually a director-level role.
  3. **Rank 2 — Specialized Team Member (Station Specialist)**: Deep expertise owner of a specific operational area with measurable performance metrics.

### Rank 3 — Team Leaders & Specialized Trainers
- Team Leader (Operational)
- Team Leader (Specialized)
- Specialized Trainer
- All at the same rank level despite being on different paths.

### Rank 4 — Assistant Director
- Assistant Operations Director

### Rank 5 — Directors
- Operations Director
- Specialty Director (e.g., Talent Director)
- Peer-level roles, both report directly to the Operator.

### Rank 6 — Reserved (Future Multi-Location)
- Ghost rank — not currently assigned.
- Reserved for a future store-level leader if the business expands to multiple locations.
- At that point, this person would be responsible for an individual store while the Operator oversees the whole organization.

### Rank 7 — Operator
- Top-level leader of the organization.
- Currently: single-store Operator.
- Future (if multi-location): oversees the entire organization across locations.
